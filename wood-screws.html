What are wood screws?

Wood screws use a coarse, deep, single-lead thread and a sharp or Type 17 point to bite quickly into timber and pull the joint tight with minimal splitting. They are essential for furniture, joinery and construction.

Common applications

  • Furniture manufacturing and flat-pack assembly
  • Joinery, cabinetry and shop-fitting
  • Decking, fencing and timber construction
  • General woodworking and carpentry

Heads, drives & points

Countersunk (CSK), Raised CSK, Pan, Bugle and Oval heads with Philips, Pozi, Torx and Square drives. A Type 17 self-drilling point is available to eliminate pilot holes in dense timber.

Finishes

Zinc, Yellow Zinc and Black Oxide / Phosphate for interior and treated-timber use.
